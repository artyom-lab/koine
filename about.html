Donate PITCH A STORY

Pronounced koin-uh-soon-eh

Even before the Covid19 pandemic, people have been leaving the church in droves. With remote services for many churches taking place for over a year and a half, the questions about church have, we believe, reached an important precipice. What is the purpose of church? What are the systemic/structural issues with the modern conception/practice of church? Why do congregants struggle to become daily disciples? What are our structures setting us up for and what can we do to improve them?

Discussions are underway to address these questions. The micro church (house church) movement is expanding rapidly in The United States. With scandal after scandal plaguing church clergy, people are rethinking what church leadership looks like.

Out of this, the idea for an online magazine, Koinesúnē, was born. Koinesúnē is the merging of two Greek words that accurately represent what we are trying to elevate, koinonia meaning fellowship and community, and dikaiosúnē which means righteousness or harmony.

The purpose of Koinesúnē is to provide a platform for discussing these essential questions at this essential time. Our aim is to find people and organizations who are trying something different. Something centered on intimacy, real community, and the complexity of disciple-making rather than an event in a set apart building once a week. And create a platform to discuss the elements surrounding true community - vulnerability, communication, shared vision, conflict, love, empowerment, service, etc...

What are we all about? Celebrating the Pursuit of Authentic Discipleship

Our Objectives:

Be a hub - source of empowerment, encouragement, and celebration - for the movement Equip the body in establishing healthy structures and maintaining Jesus-centered cultures Resource micro-churches toward Kingdom success Contribute significantly to conversation about what church is and how to be better

What do we value?

Elevating Jesus: Trusting God above our own flesh; worshipping through all circumstances; acknowledging and celebrating that God alone is God.

Risk-taking: Calculated, truth based courage. refusing to be confined by the patterns of this world. A celebration of trying.

Neighborhood Centered: True community and true discipleship are not events or brief encounters; one needs to be in life with others, consistently and sometimes without appointment for true intimacy to develop and the full value of community to manifest.

WE There (inclusive ownership of members): Everyone is a leader; everyone is a participant. Opportunity is not limited to the “skilled few”. A shared vision means all participants are valued, empowered, and celebrated as co-owners in the body of Christ.

Truth-based Reality: Seeking and sharing the truth. Not creating false narratives or alternative realities to cope with the complexities. Seeking the truth with courage.

Personal Stewardship: Discipleship starts within. We want to lead with self-assessment and a focus on what each of us can control. Participants influence one another, but stewarding one’s life is one’s own responsibility.
